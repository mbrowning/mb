Communicative Design Approach

I tend to think expansively; I can focus on a single aspect of something, but I can’t ignore how it’s connected to everything else.

Particularly, in interface design, I see usability, visual design, and marketing & branding as symbiotic parts of a communicative whole.

These seemingly discrete (or even opposing) aspects of interface design are all forms of communication, each with a primary aim:

  • Designing for usability entails providing cues that make an interface easy to understand and use.
  • Visual design communicates a feel or mood to provoke an emotional response.
  • Marketing messages contain information about what’s on offer now, and branding involves creating impressions as to what the entity is all about.

Examining the secondary effects of their messages reveals the interconnectedness of these aspects:

  • A usable interface design builds the brand by illustrating that the designers (and by extension, the entity) are paying attention and want to help.
  • Visual design has the power to make someone feel good. What could beat that for brand identity?
  • Designs that make people feel good also seem more usable, because everything is better when you’re happy.

You can examine the aspects of an interface individually, but to me, it’s not really possible to isolate them from one another.
