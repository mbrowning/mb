product & service nav edit

  • Jump to Content
  1. The drop-down (already built into the global nav) contains the three sub-categories previously presented separately: gift shop, gift cards, and gift registry.
    • If someone is looking for any of these three “gift” links, they’re first and foremost looking for the word “gift,” and they are sure to see it here.
  2. Home is the parent category for all home-related departments; these second-level categories are visible in the redesigned vertical nav.
  3. The four "people" categories are grouped together.
  4. Shoes, swim, and luggage retain their status as top-level categories; Kohl's has separate departments for all of these in their physical stores.
  5. Same for toys and sports fan shop—these are in-house specialty shops, and should be presented accordingly.
  6. Clearance keeps its spot in the global nav; this gives Kohl's specials lots of visibility, and mirrors the clearance section in the physical store.
  7. Sign in now appears in the conventional spot, the top right corner of the page.
    • Also, my account and kohl's charge have been removed; all three of these links went to the same sign in page, and users wanting to complete tasks related to their charge card or account will expect to first have to sign in anyway.
  8. Customer service moves right next door, where it's easily seen and still in the expected area of the page.
  9. Order Status is most related to customer service, so it stays to its left. Gift registry gets its own link here, as it leads to a different place than the other gift options.
  10. Store locator clearly belongs in the account/service row.
