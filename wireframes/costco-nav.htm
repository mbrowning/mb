product nav edit

  • Jump to Content
  1. Main Section
    • With so many categories, the global nav is much easier to scan vertically. This also provides plenty of space to add a few new categories (net total of four added):
    • Added
      • Crafts & Hobbies
      • Pharmacy
      • Photo
      • Office & Paper
      • Gift Cards
      • Gift Certificates
      • Memberships
      • Tickets
    • Removed
      • Entertainment
      • Office Products
      • What's New (relocated)
      • View All (relocated)
    • Crafts & Hobbies was previously under Entertainment, where it didn't really belong. Photo and Pharmacy were pulled from one of the deleted horizontal navs. All of these labels represent departments, so they belong in the global nav.
    • Office Products becomes Office & Paper, so that Invitations & Announcements, previously listed under Entertainment, can become one of its subcategories. People probably buy these items mostly from big office supply stores anyway, so this is a natural place to look.
  2. Auxiliary Section
    • The remaining four added items (beginning with Gift Cards) don't represent departments, but there's no department to put them under—they were previously subcategories of Entertainment, which made little sense. So they go in the global nav, separate from the departmental links, and clustered together to signify their commonality. This also gives these categories top-level visibility on the home page, where before they were buried or completely excluded.
    • Each of these links goes to its own page, so the content currently crammed together on the Entertainment - Tickets & Gift Certificates page can be indexed accurately and displayed attractively.
    • Memberships is the only completely new label. It was a necessary addition, as the many memberships on offer don't fit anywhere else.
    • All the subcategories that were listed under Entertainment have now been accounted for, except for Video Games. But it already appears under Electronics, which is a more conventional parent category anyway.
  3. Supplemental Nav
    • Finally, What's New and View All appear directly above the global nav, which highlights their relation to, and distinction from, the global nav's "departmental" categories. This also increases their visibility.

  1. Entertainment
    • Entertainment doesn't accurately describe its contents:
      • Crafts & Hobbies
      • Invitations & Announcements
      • Photo Center
      • Tickets & Gift Certificates
      • Video Games
    • Invitations & Announcements, Photo Center, Gift Certificates, and even Crafts & Hobbies don't really qualify as "entertainment."
    • Nor do the gift cards and membership offers listed on the Tickets & Gift Certificates page, and as they're not linked in the global nav (or anywhere else), users may never know they exist.
    • The Tickets & Gift Certificates page also has way too many types of content, which renders its page title meaningless and its content a jumbled mess.
  2. What's New & View All
    • Every category in the global nav represents a department (Furniture, Computers, Hardware) except for What's New and View All. These are actually supplementary navigation options, so they shouldn't be presented as on the same level as the departmental categories.
