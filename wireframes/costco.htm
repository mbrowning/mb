  1. Layout is barely 800 pixels wide, which is much narrower than most sites, and absurd for a big box retailer like Costco. The plethora of content has to be squished together for it all to fit.
  2. Two horizontal nav rows, each with an unclear identity, are stacked on top of one another. Visually disorienting and conceptually puzzling.
  3. Global nav is double-stacked, which is almost always a bad sign. Too many categories for effective horizontal viewing.
  4. Login is wedged between the global nav and search in an unconventional spot. Some users may never notice it here.
  5. All caps and too much text makes this link hard to look at. Glancing over it, you see "CLICK HERE" and a big jumble of words; it looks like a banner ad, so the "ignore" impulse instantly kicks in.
  6. This is way too far down the page for an "info" link, especially since it's sandwiched between ads and content. The bold font helps somewhat, but the overall approach is counterintuitive.
  7. Too many disparate, graphics-heavy promo sections packed together too tightly.
